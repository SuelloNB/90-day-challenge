How Do You Create Good Background and Foreground Contrast in Your Designs?

Good background and foreground contrast is essential for making user interfaces readable, usable, and accessible to all users. It refers to the visual difference between text (foreground) and its background, ensuring that content can be easily seen under different conditions. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define standards for contrast, where AA requires a minimum ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text, while AAA—the highest level—requires at least 7:1. Meeting these standards helps support users with visual impairments and improves overall user experience. As a developer, it’s important to prioritize readability over aesthetics, use reliable tools to check contrast ratios, and apply consistent color systems to ensure designs are both visually appealing and accessible.
