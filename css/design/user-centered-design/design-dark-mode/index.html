What Are Best Practices for Designing a Dark Mode Feature?

Designing an effective dark mode is far more nuanced than simply inverting your CSS hex codes; it requires a deliberate shift in how you handle depth and legibility. From a senior engineering perspective, the golden rule is to avoid pure black (#000000) for large surfaces to prevent "smearing" on OLED screens and excessive eye strain; instead, use deep grays and communicate elevation by using progressively lighter surface overlays rather than traditional drop shadows. You must also desaturate your primary color palette, as high-vibrancy colors that look great on white backgrounds can "bleed" and become unreadable against dark tones, potentially failing WCAG accessibility standards. Ultimately, the best implementation respects the user’s system-level preferences via the prefers-color-scheme media query while offering a manual override toggle to ensure the interface remains comfortable regardless of the ambient lighting.
